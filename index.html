Geopolitical Volatility Reshapes Global Oil Supply Chains

The global oil market faces its most complex risk environment in decades. From the Strait of Hormuz to the Red Sea, critical chokepoints that carry a significant share of world oil supply are under unprecedented threat from state and non-state actors alike.

This monitor tracks crude oil production across 30 major producing nations and assesses geopolitical risk based on current conflict conditions, sanctions regimes, and supply chain disruptions as of 2024–25.

Risk scores are computed using a multi-factor model that weighs proximity to conflict zones, international sanctions exposure, pipeline disruption status, and strategic chokepoint vulnerability.

Data Sources

Production data sourced from Kaggle crude oil production dataset (2018 figures). Risk assessments reflect 2024–25 geopolitical conditions based on open-source intelligence including UCDP conflict data, UN sanctions records, and shipping disruption reports.
